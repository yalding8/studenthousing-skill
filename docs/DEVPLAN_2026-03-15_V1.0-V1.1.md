```markdown
### Step 1 — Understand Intent & Collect Slots

Before searching, identify what the user actually needs:

**Intent A — Direct search** (user wants listings now):
- Signals: "find me...", "search for...", "帮我找...", "推荐一下...", mentions specific budget/room type

**Intent C — Knowledge question** (user asks about concepts):
- Signals: "What is en-suite?", "PBSA是什么?", "bills included 包括什么?"

**Slot collection** (same as before):
- Required: Location (university name OR city name)
- Optional: Room type, Budget, Move-in period
- If zero location: ask once — "Which university or city are you studying in?"
- Do not ask more than one follow-up question.
```

```markdown
# City Housing Guides

Load this file when the user is in the "Orientation" intent (Intent B) — they've mentioned a city or university but haven't asked for specific listings yet. Use the relevant city section to give a brief overview before searching.

---

## London

London student accommodation is the most expensive in the UK but also the most diverse.

- **Key areas for students**: Bloomsbury (near UCL/SOAS), South Kensington (near Imperial), Waterloo/Southbank (near KCL), Stratford/Mile End (near Queen Mary)
- **Price range**: £200-£420/week for en-suite; £280-£600+ for studio
- **Recommendation**: For first-year international students, PBSA in Zone 1-2 near your university is safest. En-suite is the most popular choice. Budget at least £280/week for a comfortable option near central London universities.
- **Transport tip**: If staying further out, prioritize locations near Tube/Overground stations. Zone 2-3 can save £50-100/week.

## Manchester

Manchester offers excellent value — significantly cheaper than London with a compact, walkable city centre.

- **Key areas**: City Centre (5-10 min walk to UoM/MMU), Fallowfield (15 min by bus, more social/student vibe), Salford Quays (near University of Salford)
- **Price range**: £170-£340/week for en-suite; £180-£280 for studio
- **Recommendation**: First-year international students: City Centre PBSA, en-suite, £170-£250/week. Fallowfield is cheaper but requires bus commute.
- **Local tip**: Near the University of Manchester, Oxford Road corridor has the highest concentration of PBSA. Chinatown is walking distance — relevant for Chinese students.

## Edinburgh

Compact, historic city with moderate student accommodation costs.

- **Key areas**: Old Town/Newington (near University of Edinburgh), Fountainbridge, Dalry
- **Price range**: £150-£220/week for en-suite; £190-£300 for studio
- **Recommendation**: Edinburgh is very walkable. Prioritize distance — most areas near central campus are safe and convenient.

## Sydney

Sydney is expensive but has strong PBSA options near major universities.

- **Key areas for UNSW**: Kensington, Randwick, Kingsford. For USyd: Camperdown, Newtown, Chippendale.
- **Price range**: A$300-A$450/week for en-suite; A$400-A$600 for studio
- **Recommendation**: PBSA near campus is the safest bet for first-year international students. Public transport is reliable but adds cost (Opal card ~A$50/week).

## Melbourne

More affordable than Sydney with a well-connected public transport network.

- **Key areas**: CBD (near RMIT/University of Melbourne), Clayton (near Monash main campus), Southbank
- **Price range**: A$280-A$420/week for en-suite; A$380-A$550 for studio
- **Recommendation**: CBD is ideal for University of Melbourne and RMIT students. Monash students should look at Clayton-area options.

## New York

The most expensive city for student accommodation in the US.

- **Key areas**: Greenwich Village/East Village (near NYU), Morningside Heights (near Columbia), Midtown
- **Price range**: $300-$500/week for en-suite; $500-$800+ for studio
- **Recommendation**: NYC housing is competitive. Book early (3-6 months ahead). Shared apartments are common and more affordable.

## Toronto

Canada's largest student city with growing PBSA supply.

- **Key areas**: Downtown Core (near UofT), North York (near York University)
- **Price range**: C$250-C$380/week for en-suite; C$350-C$550 for studio
- **Recommendation**: Downtown is priciest but most convenient. Transit (TTC) is reliable for commuting from affordable neighborhoods.

## Birmingham

Affordable UK city with a strong student community.

- **Key areas**: Selly Oak (traditional UoB student area), City Centre (near Aston/BCU)
- **Price range**: £120-£180/week for en-suite; £160-£250 for studio
- **Recommendation**: Great value compared to London. Selly Oak has the most student-focused vibe.
```

```markdown
# Housing Decision Guide

Load this file when the user needs help choosing between room types, is unsure about their needs, or has specific lifestyle preferences. Use these frameworks to give personalised recommendations.

---

## Room Type Decision Framework

### Quick Recommendation by Profile

| User profile | Recommended room type | Why |
|-------------|----------------------|-----|
| First-year international student, wants to meet people | **En-suite** | Private bathroom + shared kitchen = best balance of privacy and social life |
| Values maximum privacy, studies from home a lot | **Studio** | Self-contained, no shared spaces |
| Budget-conscious, okay with sharing | **Non-en-suite** or **Shared room** | Cheapest options |
| Couple or needs extra space | **1-Bed apartment** | Separate living area |
| Group of friends booking together | **2-Bed / 3-Bed** | Split cost, live with friends |

### Decision by Lifestyle

| "I am..." | Suggestion |
|-----------|-----------|
| "社恐 / introverted" | Studio — you won't need to share any space |
| "I love cooking Chinese food" | Studio (own kitchen) or check if PBSA has good communal kitchen ventilation |
| "I want to bring my pet" | Submit demand form — standard PBSA typically doesn't allow pets |
| "I need wheelchair access" | Submit demand form — uhomes advisors can find accessible units |
| "I want to save money" | Non-en-suite in a slightly further location. Also check for discounts and "no deposit" properties |
| "Safety is my top priority" | PBSA with 24h security + CCTV. Avoid isolated private rentals |

## Budget Guidance

### "Is my budget realistic?"

When a user states a budget, compare it against the city's typical range:

- **Budget is within typical range** → Confirm it's reasonable, proceed with search
- **Budget is 10-20% below minimum** → "Your budget is a bit tight for [city]. You might find [non-en-suite / shared room] options, or consider areas slightly further from campus. Want me to search with relaxed filters?"
- **Budget is >20% below minimum** → "£[X]/week is significantly below the typical range for [city] ([range]). I'd recommend either adjusting your budget or looking at shared rooms. You could also submit a demand form and let a uhomes advisor find creative options for you."

### First-year vs Returning Student

| | First-year | Returning student |
|--|-----------|-------------------|
| Recommended type | PBSA (all-inclusive, managed, social) | PBSA or private rental (more flexibility) |
| Contract length | Full year (44-51 weeks) | Semester or flexible if available |
| Key priority | Close to campus, safe, bills included | Value for money, specific amenities |
```

### DEV-14: 语言处理规则升级 [评审报告 Part IV 专家 2 建议 4]

**变更文件**：`uhomes-student-housing/SKILL.md` Language Handling 段落

**当前规则**：
```
- If the user writes in Chinese: respond in Chinese throughout.
- If the user writes in English: respond in English.
```

**新规则**：
```markdown
## Language Handling

- Respond in the user's **primary language** (the language that makes up the majority of their message).
- Mixed-language input (e.g. "帮我看看 Manchester 的 en-suite") → respond in the primary language (Chinese in this example).
- **Property names, room type terms (en-suite, studio, PBSA), and city/university names stay in their original English form** regardless of response language. These are proper nouns or industry-standard terms that translate poorly.
- Currency symbols and prices always use the local format (£, A$, $, C$).
```

**验收标准**：
- 混合语言输入时能正确判断主要语言
- 专有名词保持英文
- 规则简洁（不超过 5 条）
