# Housing Decision Guide

Load this file when the user needs help choosing between room types, is unsure about their needs, or has specific lifestyle preferences. Use these frameworks to give personalised recommendations.

---

## 1. Room Type Decision Framework

Quick recommendation based on user profile:

| User Profile | Recommended Room Type | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First-year social student | En-suite | Private bathroom for comfort, shared kitchen for socialising with flatmates |
| Privacy-focused / introverted | Studio | Fully self-contained, no shared spaces, maximum personal space |
| Budget-conscious student | Non-en-suite (shared bathroom) | Lowest price point while still living in quality PBSA accommodation |
| Couple | 1-bed apartment | Dedicated living space designed for two people, private kitchen and bathroom |
| Friend group (2-3 people) | 2-bed or 3-bed apartment | Share rent to save money, live with people you already know and trust |
| Postgrad / mature student | Studio or 1-bed | Quieter environment, independence, better suited to focused study routines |
| Short-stay / summer student | En-suite or studio | Flexible booking periods, no need to furnish or set up utilities |

## 3. Budget Guidance

When the user asks "Is my budget realistic?", assess against the typical price range for their target city and room type:

### Within typical range

The budget fits comfortably within the expected price range for the chosen city and room type.

**Action:** Confirm the budget is realistic, proceed to show matching properties, and highlight any that offer good value within their range.

### 10-20% below minimum

The budget is slightly below the typical starting price for their preferred room type or area.

**Action:** Be transparent that options at this price are limited, then suggest practical alternatives:
- Switch from en-suite to non-en-suite (shared bathroom) to reduce cost
- Consider properties in areas slightly further from the city centre or campus
- Ask about available discounts (early-bird booking, longer tenancy, or seasonal promotions)
- Check if any properties include bills in the rent (which may make the effective cost more competitive)

### More than 20% below minimum

The budget is significantly below the typical price range for the target city.

**Action:** Be honest and direct — do not set unrealistic expectations. Then offer constructive next steps:
- Explain that this budget is well below the typical range for the chosen city
- Suggest shared rooms (twin rooms or shared apartments) if available
- Recommend submitting a demand form so a uhomes advisor can search for any special deals, newly listed properties, or alternative options that may not appear on the main platform
- If the user is flexible on city, suggest more affordable alternatives (e.g., smaller cities or towns with lower living costs)

## 4. First-year vs Returning Student

| Factor | First-year Student | Returning Student |
|---|---|---|
| **Recommended accommodation** | PBSA (en-suite or studio) | PBSA, private rental, or shared apartment |
| **Why PBSA for first years** | All-inclusive bills, no setup hassle, built-in social community, close to campus, safety and security | Already familiar with the city, may prefer more independence or lower cost |
| **Social needs** | High — meeting new people is a priority; shared flats and communal areas help | Variable — some want to live with existing friends, others want quiet |
| **Location priority** | Close to campus and city centre (still learning the area) | May be comfortable living further out for lower rent if they know the transport |
| **Contract length** | Typically 40-51 weeks (full academic year) | May prefer shorter or more flexible contracts |
| **Budget awareness** | Often less experienced with budgeting; all-inclusive is simpler | Better understanding of costs; may want to optimise spending |
| **Key advice** | Book early — first-year PBSA fills up fast, especially en-suites in popular cities | Compare PBSA vs private rental prices; use the demand form for personalised options |
