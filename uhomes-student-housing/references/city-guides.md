# City Housing Guides

Load this file when the user is in the "Orientation" intent (Intent B) — they've mentioned a city or university but haven't asked for specific listings yet. Use the relevant city section to give a brief overview before searching.

## London

**Key areas for students:**
- **King's Cross / Islington** — Close to UCL, SOAS, and multiple tube lines; has a large student community with plenty of cafes and libraries nearby.
- **South Kensington / Earl's Court** — Convenient for Imperial College students; well-connected by Piccadilly and District lines.
- **Stratford / Mile End** — Popular with Queen Mary students; more affordable than central zones and directly on the Jubilee and Central lines.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: £200–£420/week
- Studio: £280–£600+/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议优先考虑学校附近的 purpose-built student accommodation (PBSA)，包含bills的房型能省去自己开通水电网的麻烦，适合刚到英国的同学。

**Transport tip:**
Get an 18+ Student Oyster card for 30% off Travelcard season tickets. Budget around £140–£160/month for Zones 1–3 travel.

## Manchester

**Key areas for students:**
- **Fallowfield** — The most established student neighbourhood in Manchester; has a large student community and is close to the University of Manchester campus.
- **Oxford Road Corridor** — Runs between the two main universities (UoM and Manchester Met); walking distance to lectures and the city centre.
- **Salford Quays / MediaCityUK** — A good option for University of Salford students; modern developments along the waterfront with tram access to the city centre.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: £170–£340/week
- Studio: £180–£280/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议选择 Oxford Road 沿线的学生公寓，步行即可到校，周边超市、餐厅齐全，生活非常方便。

**Local tip:**
Manchester's bus network (Bee Network) covers most student areas. A termly bus pass is often cheaper than weekly tickets — check for student deals at the start of term.

## Edinburgh

**Key areas for students:**
- **Newington / Marchmont** — Traditional student quarter close to the University of Edinburgh's central campus; has a large student community and plenty of independent shops.
- **Fountainbridge / Tollcross** — Slightly more affordable, walkable to both Edinburgh Napier and University of Edinburgh; good pub and café scene.
- **Leith Walk** — A short bus ride from the city centre; growing number of PBSA options and a more local neighbourhood feel.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: £150–£220/week
- Studio: £190–£300/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议住在 Newington 或 Marchmont 一带，走路就能到学校，周围中超和亚洲餐厅也比较多，生活上容易适应。

**Local tip:**
Edinburgh is compact enough to walk or cycle most days. A Lothian Buses student ridacard (around £50/month) covers unlimited travel within the city.

## Sydney

**Key areas for students:**
- **Ultimo / Chippendale** — Close to UTS and University of Sydney; has a large student community, with Central Station and Broadway Shopping Centre nearby.
- **Kensington / Kingsford** — The go-to area for UNSW students; connected to the city by light rail and multiple bus routes.
- **Redfern / Surry Hills** — A vibrant inner-city area within walking distance of several universities; lots of food options and cultural venues.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: A$300–A$450/week
- Studio: A$400–A$600/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议选择学校步行范围内的学生公寓，悉尼公共交通费用较高，住得近能省下不少通勤开支。

**Transport tip:**
Get an Opal card and register for the Transport Concession Entitlement through your university. Off-peak and weekend travel is capped at A$8.90/day.

## Melbourne

**Key areas for students:**
- **Carlton / Parkville** — The University of Melbourne's home turf; has a large student community and is famous for Lygon Street's cafes and restaurants.
- **CBD (City Centre)** — Central to RMIT, multiple TAFE colleges, and well-connected tram routes; a practical choice if you study at different campuses.
- **Footscray / West Footscray** — More affordable than inner suburbs; growing food scene and a quick train ride to the CBD and Victoria University.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: A$280–A$420/week
- Studio: A$380–A$550/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议优先看 Carlton 和 CBD 区域的学生公寓，墨尔本市中心电车免费区（Free Tram Zone）覆盖了大部分校区和生活设施，出行非常方便。

**Local tip:**
Melbourne's Free Tram Zone covers the entire CBD and Docklands area — no Myki card needed within the zone. If you live and study inside it, transport costs can be close to zero.

## New York

**Key areas for students:**
- **Morningside Heights / Harlem** — Columbia University's neighbourhood; has a large student community, good subway access (1/A/B/C lines), and more affordable dining than Midtown.
- **Greenwich Village / East Village** — Close to NYU; lively atmosphere with plenty of student-friendly restaurants and nightlife, though rents are higher.
- **Downtown Brooklyn / Fort Greene** — A more affordable alternative with easy subway access to Manhattan campuses; growing number of student housing options.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: $300–$500/week
- Studio: $500–$800+/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议选择学校官方合作的宿舍或 PBSA，纽约租房市场竞争激烈且流程复杂（需要 SSN、担保人等），入学第一年住学生公寓能避免很多麻烦。

**Transport tip:**
An unlimited MetroCard is $33/week or $127/month and covers all subway and local bus routes. Full-time students at some universities can get discounted transit passes.

## Toronto

**Key areas for students:**
- **Annex / Harbord Village** — Right next to the University of Toronto's St. George campus; has a large student community with bookshops, cafes, and easy TTC subway access.
- **Dundas West / Little Portugal** — A bit further west but more affordable; trendy neighbourhood with good streetcar connections to UofT and OCAD.
- **North York (Yonge & Finch area)** — Popular with students at York University and Seneca College; a large East Asian food scene and direct subway line downtown.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: C$250–C$380/week
- Studio: C$350–C$550/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议第一年选择 campus 附近的学生公寓，多伦多冬天很冷（常到零下十几度），住得离学校近能减少户外通勤时间。

**Local tip:**
A TTC monthly pass costs around C$156 and gives unlimited rides on subway, bus, and streetcar. Post-secondary students can get a discounted pass through their student union — check during orientation.

## Birmingham

**Key areas for students:**
- **Selly Oak** — The classic University of Birmingham student area; has a large student community, affordable rent, and is right next to campus with its own train station.
- **Digbeth / Eastside** — Close to Birmingham City University and Aston University; an up-and-coming creative district with good nightlife.
- **Edgbaston** — A quieter residential area between the city centre and UoB; popular with postgraduate students who prefer a calmer environment.

**Price range:**
- En-suite: £120–£180/week
- Studio: £160–£250/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议住在 Selly Oak 的学生公寓，性价比在英国主要城市中属于很高的，走路到 UoB 校区只需几分钟。

**Local tip:**
Birmingham has an expanding tram (Metro) network connecting the city centre with outer areas. A 16–18 Saver or 26–30 Railcard also works on West Midlands trains for off-peak discounts.

## Dublin

**Key areas for students:**
- **Rathmines / Ranelagh** — A popular student neighbourhood south of the city centre; has a large student community and is within cycling distance of UCD, Trinity, and most city-centre colleges.
- **Drumcondra** — Close to DCU's campus; quieter and more affordable than the south side, with good bus links into town.
- **Smithfield / Stoneybatter** — A centrally located area on the north side; walkable to TU Dublin and within easy reach of other institutions via Luas (tram).

**Price range:**
- En-suite: €200–€320/week
- Studio: €280–€450/week

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
建议尽早申请学生公寓，都柏林住房市场非常紧张，特别是每年 8–9 月高峰期。如果学校提供 first-year guarantee，建议优先使用。

**Local tip:**
Get a Student Leap Card for discounted fares on Dublin Bus, Luas, and DART. Cycling is very practical in Dublin — the Bleeper bike-share scheme offers affordable monthly plans for students.

## Tokyo

**Key areas for students:**
- **Shinjuku / Takadanobaba** — Popular with Waseda University students; vibrant area with many restaurants, shops, and excellent train connections.
- **Bunkyo / Hongo** — Close to University of Tokyo's main campus; quieter residential area with a strong academic atmosphere.
- **Meguro / Ota** — Near Tokyo Institute of Technology (Ookayama campus); more residential with good access to Tokyu lines.

**Price range:**
- En-suite (individual unit): ¥80,000–¥150,000/month (~£400–£750/month)
- Studio (ワンルーム): ¥60,000–¥120,000/month (~£300–£600/month)
- Shared house/シェアハウス: ¥40,000–¥80,000/month (~£200–£400/month)

**Recommendation for first-year international students:**
初めて日本に来る留学生には、家具付きの学生マンションやシェアハウスがおすすめです。敷金・礼金（deposit + key money）が不要な物件を探すと初期費用を抑えられます。大学の留学生課で紹介される物件も確認してみてください。

**Local tip:**
Get a student commuter pass (通学定期券) for significant savings on daily train travel. A Suica or Pasmo IC card is essential. Note that Japanese rental contracts often require a guarantor (保証人) — many student housing providers offer guarantor services for international students.
